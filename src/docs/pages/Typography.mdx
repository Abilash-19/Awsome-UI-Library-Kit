Typography is used to maintain visual hierarchy and readability throughout the application.

## Display Variants
Use display variants for large, impactful text like hero headlines.

<ComponentPreview code={`<Typography variant="display1">Display 1</Typography>
<Typography variant="display2">Display 2</Typography>`}>
  <div className="space-y-4">
    <Typography variant="display1">Display 1</Typography>
    <Typography variant="display2">Display 2</Typography>
  </div>
</ComponentPreview>

## Body & Support
Text styles for paragraphs, captions, and overlines.

<ComponentPreview code={`<Typography variant="body1">Body 1: The standard paragraph style.</Typography>
<Typography variant="body2">Body 2: A slightly smaller paragraph style.</Typography>
<Typography variant="caption">Caption: Smallest text for meta info.</Typography>`}>
  <div className="space-y-4 flex flex-col">
    <Typography variant="body1">Body 1: The standard paragraph style.</Typography>
    <Typography variant="body2">Body 2: A slightly smaller paragraph style.</Typography>
    <Typography variant="caption">Caption: Smallest text for meta info.</Typography>
  </div>
</ComponentPreview>

## Custom Weights
You can override the default weight of any variant.

<ComponentPreview code={`<Typography variant="h4" weight="bold">Bold H4</Typography>
<Typography variant="h4" weight="thin">Thin H4</Typography>`}>
  <div className="space-y-4">
    <Typography variant="h4" weight="bold">Bold H4</Typography>
    <Typography variant="h4" weight="thin">Thin H4</Typography>
  </div>
</ComponentPreview>

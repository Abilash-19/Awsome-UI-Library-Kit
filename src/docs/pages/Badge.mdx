Badges are compact status indicators that highlight metadata, status, or counts. They are designed to stand out without disrupting the layout flow.

## Basic Usage
Use badges to represent states like progress, success, or urgency.

<ComponentPreview code={`<Badge variant="primary">Updated</Badge>
<Badge variant="success">Online</Badge>
<Badge variant="danger">High Priority</Badge>`}>
  <div className="flex flex-wrap gap-4 items-center">
    <Badge variant="primary">New Feature</Badge>
    <Badge variant="success">System Online</Badge>
    <Badge variant="warning">Maintenance</Badge>
    <Badge variant="danger">Security Risk</Badge>
  </div>
</ComponentPreview>

## Customization
### Sizes
Badges come in three sizes to fit different contexts.

<ComponentPreview code={`<Badge size="small">Small</Badge>
<Badge size="medium">Medium</Badge>
<Badge size="large">Large</Badge>`}>
  <div className="flex items-center gap-4">
    <Badge size="small">Small Label</Badge>
    <Badge size="medium">Standard Badge</Badge>
    <Badge size="large">Hero Badge</Badge>
  </div>
</ComponentPreview>

### Outline Style
For a more subtle look, use the `outline` variant.

<ComponentPreview code={`<Badge variant="outline">Secondary</Badge>`}>
  <div className="flex gap-4">
    <Badge variant="outline">Draft</Badge>
    <Badge variant="outline">Archived</Badge>
  </div>
</ComponentPreview>

### Loading State
Badges support a built-in skeleton state for when data is still fetching.

<ComponentPreview code={`<Badge isLoading>Placeholder</Badge>`}>
  <div className="flex gap-4">
    <Badge isLoading>Loading...</Badge>
    <Badge isLoading size="large">Large Loading</Badge>
  </div>
</ComponentPreview>
